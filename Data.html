The growth of any investment depends primarily on three interconnected factors: the initial amount, the interest rate, and the number of years the money remains invested. The initial amount, or principal, forms the base on which all returns are calculated, meaning larger starting sums naturally generate greater earnings over time. The interest rate determines how quickly the investment grows, with higher rates producing faster accumulation, especially when compound interest is involved, since it applies interest to both the principal and previously earned interest. The number of years invested amplifies this effect, as longer time horizons allow compounding to work more powerfully, often resulting in exponential growth. Together, these factors shape the future value of an investment, making it essential for investors to understand how each one influences overall returns and how adjusting any of them can significantly impact long‑term financial outcomes.
